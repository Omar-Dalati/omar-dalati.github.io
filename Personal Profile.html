Personal Profile

What do the results of these tests mean for you? After analysing the information regarding my test about myself, I noticed a correlation between only two of them. The results of the Myers-Briggs personality test have determined that I am INFJ-T which stands for Introverted, Intuitive, Feeling and Judging. I am someone who thinks with my emotions more so than a logical perspective. However, this doesn't mean I won't believe logically whatsoever. I am principled and work in a manner where I need to have a concise set of plans before I can get working, even though, at the same time I'm a hands-on and enjoy learning as I go, which in the second test I chose was Education Planner. The result that came from Education Planner was that I am someone who learns hands-on. While creating my website for this assignment, I enjoyed coding with what looked the best to me, and I continued to do so. However, I still had a set layout within the back of my mind and worked on that basis before jumping in directly.

Honey and Mumford's test stated that I am a reflector, and reflectors prefer to stand back and observe before jumping into it. These are now two very different results from firstly me being hands-on to now being reluctant and staying around. The truth is that I enjoy working with the information that I find, then with the information that I have gathered I start the project.

How do you think these results may influence your behaviour in a team? The results may influence the behaviour of the team in that I am someone who likes to work alone. As you can see, I am introverted; however, where I do have to work with others, I will do so and participate. I am someone who enjoys having a set-out plan of the work before jumping into the work directly and planning out a scenario of how we will work together as a team, and now in a team setting, I want to make sure all of us are on the same boat, and I know sometimes we won't be, but that's fine as we can step back and plan out a workaround in the instance this does occur. This is how my results may influence my behaviour in a team.

How should you take this into account when forming a team? The best way to consider this is that I prefer being hands-on when working with a team. For example, I like being a leader, but at the same time, I like being designated a position/role and told what to do. However, what my team and I can do as an example is determine everyone's strengths and weaknesses and for everyone within their results, which could assist us in seeing how we learn and think and work.
